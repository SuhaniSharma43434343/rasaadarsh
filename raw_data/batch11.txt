201. Shwasahara Vataka (श्वासहरवटक)


Sanskrit Shlokas
Shlokas 54–57
Saadhaarannatu vattakam vakshyaami shrinnu tattvatah | Paaradam gandhakashchaiva palamekam prithak prithak ||54|| Palatrayam trikatukam vangamekappalam kshipet | Sarvmekatra samyojya dinaani treeni mardayet ||55|| Moortrena cha tathaa treeni dinaani parimardayet | Akshhapramaanavattakam chaayaashushkanttu kaarayet ||56|| Nityamekanntu vattakam dinaani trimshadeva cha | Shvaasaasajvarharamagnimanndyaarcuchiprannut ||57||
I shall now describe a general Vataka (pill) — listen well. Take: Parada (mercury) 1 Pala (46 gm) and Gandhaka (sulphur) 1 Pala (46 gm) — separately. Add Trikatu (Shunthi+Maricha+Pippali) 3 Pala (138 gm total) and Vanga (tin) 1 Pala (46 gm). Combine all together and triturate for 3 days in a mortar. Then triturate again with Mutra (Gomutra/cow's urine) for 3 more days. Form pills of Aksha (1 Karsha = 12 gm) size — but in today's practice make 375 mg pills. Dry in shade and store in a glass vessel. Take 1 pill morning and evening with Vasa Patra Svaras (Adhatoda vasica leaf juice) and Madhu (honey). In 30 days, Shwas Roga (respiratory distress/asthma), Kasa (cough), Jwara (fever), Agnimandya (low digestive fire), and Aruchi (loss of appetite/taste) are cured. This is called "Shwasahara Vataka".

Ingredients (Ghatak)

Ingredient (Sanskrit)	English Name	Quantity
Shu. Parada (शु. पारद)	Purified Mercury	50 gm (1 Pala)
Shu. Gandhaka (शु. गन्धक)	Purified Sulphur	50 gm (1 Pala)
Shunthi Churna (शुण्ठीचूर्ण)	Dry Ginger powder (part of Trikatu)	50 gm
Pippali Churna (पिप्पलीचूर्ण)	Long Pepper powder (part of Trikatu)	50 gm
Maricha Churna (मरिचचूर्ण)	Black Pepper powder (part of Trikatu)	50 gm

Gomutra (गोमूत्र)	Cow's urine — for 3-day secondary trituration	As required
Vasa Patra Svaras (वासापत्रस्वरस)	Adhatoda vasica leaf juice — Anupana	As required
Madhu (मधु)	Honey — Anupana	As required

Method of Preparation (Vidhi)
Vidhi – In a stone mortar, firmly triturate Parada and Gandhaka together to form good Kajjali. Then add all other substances (Shunthi Churna, Maricha Churna, Pippali Churna, Vanga Bhasma) and triturate all together for 3 days. Then add Gomutra (cow's urine) and triturate for another 3 days. Classical pill size: 12 gm (Aksha/1 Karsha); modern preparation: 375 mg pills. Dry in shade and store in a glass vessel. Take 1 pill morning and evening with Vasa Patra Svaras (Adhatoda vasica/malabar nut leaf juice) and Madhu (honey) for 30 days. This cures Shwas Roga (bronchial asthma/breathing difficulty), Kasa (cough), Jwara (fever), Agnimandya (weak digestion), and Aruchi (anorexia/loss of taste).

Properties

Vehicle (Anupana)	Vasa Patra Svaras (Adhatoda vasica leaf juice) with Madhu (honey)
Smell (Gandha)	Gomutra-Gandhi (cow's urine smell)
Color (Varna)	Krishna (Black)
Taste (Svad)	Katu (Pungent)
Indication (Upayoga)	Kasa (cough) and Shwas (asthma/breathing disorders)

Keep in strong direct sunlight (Teekhna Dhup) for 1 day. When the lemon juice has dissolved and absorbed the copper (Tamra Dravibhuta), add Shu. Parada 1 Tola (12 gm) and triturate again until a well-incorporated Kajjali is formed. Store in a glass vessel — this is Udayamartanda Ras. Optionallyperform Mardana procedure with water to form 125 mg pills. Take 1 Vati with water for Shotha (oedema) and Bhagandara (anal fistula).
Vimarsha (Commentary) – A similar formulation called Tamradrutiras appears in Chapter 18 of the same text. That text also states that Kajjali applied on Tamra Patra and kept in strong sunlight causes Tamra Druti (copper dissolution). However, in practice this does not fully occur by sunlight alone. Therefore, the copper-foil-coated Kajjali must be repeatedly fired (8–10 times) in Sharava Samputa (sealed clay vessel) to properly form proper Tamra Bhasma before the medicine is fit for consumption. Puta firing is therefore essential.

Color (Varna)	Krishna (Black)
Taste (Svad)	Amla (Sour)
Indication (Upayoga)	Shotha (oedema/swelling) and Bhagandara (anal fistula)

230. Vinodavidyadhara Ras (विनोदविद्याधररस)


Sanskrit Shlokas
Shlokas 4–5
Rasendrabilitatannkunnaih sajayapaalabeejaih samaih rasah sumaridito bhavetakhalu vinodavidyaadharah | Prayogudayuto haretsakalaarechaneeyyaamayaan jvaram cha jattharaamayaanudagadam sashooolam nrinaami ||4|| Samyagvirecanhaabhaave mudgakaatham pibedanu | Bhedadhikye piibettakram barbooranaaam tvaco rasam ||5||
Take: Rasendra (Shu. Parada/purified mercury), Bili/Tankana (Shu. Tankana/purified borax), and Shu. Jayapala Bija (purified Croton tiglium seeds) — all in equal parts. Firmly triturate all in a mortar. Form 125 mg pills with water (Jala Bhavana). Dry in shade. This is Vinodavidyadhara Ras. Given with Payo Guda (milk with jaggery), it removes all Rechaneeya Roga (conditions requiring purgation), Jwara (fever), Jatharamaya (gastric/abdominal diseases), Udagada (upward-moving disorders), and Shoola (pain/colic). If proper Virechana (therapeutic purgation) is not achieved, follow with Mudga Kwatha (green gram decoction). If more Bheda (purgative action) is needed, give Takra (buttermilk) or Barbura Tvak Svarasa (Acacia nilotica bark juice).
