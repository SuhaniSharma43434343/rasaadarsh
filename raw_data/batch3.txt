26. Kshudha Vati Gutika (क्षुधावती गुटिका)

This is the method of Shodhana (purification) and Bhasma-making of Abhraka.

Key Information
This section (Shlokas 8-10) describes the traditional method of making Krishna Abhraka Bhasma (purified black Incinerated Mica) — which is the primary ingredient of Kshudha Vati Gutika. The process involves:
- Soaking Krishna Abhraka (black mica) overnight in freshly prepared Dhaan ki Kanji (rice fermented water)
- Morning: Triturate in the same Kanji
- Give Bhavana (trituration) with juices of 11 medicinal plants: Manakanda, Hadjod, Khandakarna (Kharkona), Chaulai, Shalincha Shaak, Kale Marsa, Shweta Punarnava, Badi Kateri, Bhangra, Lakshmana and Keshraj
- Give Puta (firing in sealed furnace) many times — repeating this trituration and firing cycle
- Continue until Abhraka becomes Nishchandra Bhasma — i.e., the final ash has no mica-like sheen, is completely pure and fully processed

27. Leela Vilas Ras (लीलाविलासरस)

Shloka 206
Anena bhaavayetsarvam poorvam sootam punah punah | Panchavimshativaaaram cha taavaddbhrinjairairdravai ||206||
Give 25 Bhavanas (triturations) of the above prepared mixture with this Trifala Kwath, repeatedly processing it. Then give 25 more Bhavanas with Bhringaraja (Eclipta alba) juice.

Varnaga is defined in Rasa Ratna Samucchaya (8/41) as: Take equal parts of Tiksna Loha (sharp iron) and Neelanjana (lead sulphide/Galena). Melt repeatedly in a Musha (crucible). This produces a soft, black, quick-melting alloy called Varnaga. Quote: 'Teekshnaneelanjanopetem dhaatnm hi bahusho dridhm. Mridu krishnam drutadraavam varnaagam taduchyate.'

Method of Preparation — Summary
This is an extremely complex preparation involving multiple stages:

Stage 5: Make Kajjali from the final Bhasma with Shu. Parada. Add Vana Surana, Nirgundi, Maharashtri and other herb juices — triturate. Give 3x Bhavana with Maarkava (Eclipta alba) juice.

Scholarly Commentary (Vimarsha)
Acharya Sri stated the dose as 'Mashadvaym' (two Masha = 2 gm) which is too high. The author has reduced this to 1 Ratti (125 mg) which is appropriate. For Kapha-type Arsha: there is more swelling in the anal region. For Vidapana-type Arsha (prolapsed): there is even more swelling.

Vimarsha — Scholarly Commentary on 'Rasagrasta Gandhaka'
The term 'Rasagrasta Samudreerna Gandhakasya' refers to: Gandhaka that falls/settles during the Jarana (absorption) process of Parada. Alternatively, it refers to Gandhaka that settles at the bottom of the glass bottle (Kupi) when making Rasa Sindura. However, the author notes that both interpretations are impractical today since: (1) Parada Jarana is not done nowadays, and (2) no Gandhaka is found at the bottom of the Kupi when making Rasa Sindura. Therefore, the author recommends simply taking 3 Pala (140 gm) of Shu. Gandhaka as the ingredient.
