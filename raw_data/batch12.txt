Method: First prepare kajjali from Parada and Gandhaka. Add Mana:shila and Tuttha; perform Mardana procedure. Add all remaining Bhasmas and ingredients; perform Mardana procedure well. Give Lemon juice (Nimbu svaras) overnight. In the morning perform Mardana procedure again. Apply this thick paste to a copper bowl (1 Pala = 46g weight copper). Place the paste-coated copper bowl upside-down (Adhomukha) inside a small earthen pot (Haandi). Cover with a flat lid (Sharaava) and seal (Sandhiabandha). Fill the empty portion of the Haandi with ash from cow-dung cakes. Close the Haandi mouth with Sharaava-Samput. Dry. Place the entire pot on a stove and cook for 2 days and nights. When completely cool, open the Samput and remove the copper bowl. Perform Mardana procedure the copper bowl together with the medicinal material. Make fine powder. Store in glass vessel. This is Apasmara Nashana Rasa. Administration: Give 375 mg daily in the morning mixed with: Vacha (Acorus calamus), Shunthee (dry ginger), Maricha (black pepper), Pippali (long pepper) and Vidanga (Embelia ribes) churna. Take with Honey. Anupana 1.5 hours later: Bakri Mutra (goat's urine). Nasya (nasal drops): Take the same herbs — Vacha, Shunthee, Maricha, Pippali, Vidanga — each 1 Karsha = 10g each; Sarso tail (mustard oil) 4 times = 16g = 760 ml; water 3 ml. Cook in iron kadahi until water portion evaporates. Filter when cool. Give 3–4 drops of this oil as Nasya (nasal drops). Both the internal medicine and Nasya oil treats significantly Apasmara (epilepsy).

249. Vijaya Bhairav Taila - 1 (विजयभैरवतैल - १)


Chapter: Vata Vyadhi Chikitsa. Classical number [26]. Shloka number 148. A medicated oil preparation. Indicated for Kampavata (tremor / Parkinson's-type disease) and all Vata Roga — used both internally (10 drops in warm milk) and externally (body application).

Sanskrit Shloka
Shloka 148
Dhanyaaamliipishttasurabhitrayasootaliiptataila kkadeeptapatavarttimukhaaatpravrittam | Kampottaraan jayati paanivilepa naabhyaam vaatamaayaan vijayabhairavanam tailam ||148||

Take equal parts of: Surabhitraya (the three fragrant minerals) — meaning Shu. Gandhaka (purified sulphur), Shu. Haratala (purified Orpiment) and Shu. Mana:shila (purified Realgar) — and Shu. Parada (purified mercury). Method: Prepare kajjali from Parada + Gandhaka. Add Haratala and Mana:shila.perform Mardana procedure  for 1 more day.perform Mardana procedure  again with Kanji (sour fermented liquid / Dhaanyaamli). Make a paste (Kalka). Take a new cloth — size according to the quantity of paste: if small quantity use 1/4 metre cloth; if large quantity use 1 metre. Cut the 1 metre cloth into 4 pieces. Apply a thin coat of the paste on each cloth piece. Roll into a long wick (Batti) shape. Tie with thread at intervals. After slight drying, immerse the wick in a vessel full of Tila Taila (sesame oil) overnight. Next day at 10 AM, take the wick with tongs (Sandasi) and hold one end in flame — light the other end. The oil drops dripping from the burning wick — collect in a vessel. When the entire wick has burned, filter the collected oil through a cloth. When cool, store in glass vessel. This oil is named Vijaya Bhairav Taila. Administration: Drink 10 drops in warm milk (Garam Dudh mein 10 bund) for internal use. Apply on the body externally. Benefits Kampavata (tremors) and all Vata Roga.
