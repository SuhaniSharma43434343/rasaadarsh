1. Anilarir Ras (अनिलारिरस)


Sanskrit Shloka
Shloka 52
Aamanilasyaasya raso'nilaarishchairaandatailena sakousikena | Katutrayenaapi sagandakena vallaikamaanam parishevayeta ||52||
The Ras (formulation) for Aamanila (Aamvata) is called Anilarir. It should be consumed in the measure of one Valla, along with Eranda Taila (castor oil), Kushtha (Saussuraea costus), the three pungents (Trikatu), and Gandhaka (sulphur).

Ingredients (Ghatak)
Ingredient (Sanskrit)	English Name	Quantity
Eranda Taila (एरण्डतैल)	Castor Oil	1 part
Shu. Guggulu (शु. गुग्गुलु)	Purified Guggul resin (Commiphora mukul)	1 part
Trikatu Churna (त्रिकटुचूर्ण)	Ginger + Black Pepper + Long Pepper powder	1 part
Shu. Gandhak Churna (शु. गन्धकचूर्ण)	Purified Sulphur powder	1 part

Method of Preparation (Vidhi)
1. Dissolve Shuddha Guggulu in warm water.
2. Add Trikatu Churna and Shuddha Gandhak Churna and mix well.
3. Transfer to a perform Mardana procedure and add Eranda Taila (castor oil).
4. Triturate thoroughly until well combined.
5. Form tablets (Vati) of 375 mg each and dry in shade. Store in a glass container.
6. This preparation is known as Anilarir Ras.

Therapeutic Benefits
By regular consumption of this formulation, Aamvata Rog (Rheumatoid Arthritis) is completely destroyed.

Properties
Property	Value
Dose (Matra)	375 mg
Vehicle (Anupana)	As directed
Indication (Upayoga)	Aamvata (Rheumatoid Arthritis)
 
2. Prabhavati Vati (प्रभावतीवटी)


Sanskrit Shlokas
Shloka 104
Hemabhraalakateekshnataapyakamalaamesham samam saptakam sootam dvigunam vishodhanabadhoosrugavishobhaajanaat | Paathaashooranasinduvaaravijayaira ndadvairmardditam tailam kaankinagandhyakam katu bhavet kalkam vatim kalpayeta ||104||
Take equal parts of seven ingredients — Svarna (gold), Abhraka (mica), Taikshna loha, Tamra (copper), Kamala (lotus), Amesha, and Parada (mercury, double quantity) — purified properly. Triturate with oils from Patha, Surana, Sinduvara, Vijaya and Eranda to make a pungent paste. Form tablets from this paste.
Shloka 105
Prabhavateeti kathitaassarkadraavenishevitaa | Tatashchaanu pibettoyam dashamoolaprasaadhitam ||105||
This is called Prabhavati — to be taken with honey/sugar vehicle. After taking it, drink water processed with Dashamula decoction.
Shloka 106
Sapippallikam pibato jalam jayeta | Marudvikaaraanudraanyapasmritam ||106||
Drinking water mixed with Pippali (long pepper) conquers Vata disorders and Apasmara (epilepsy).

Method of Preparation (Vidhi)

3. Tryambakeshwar Ras (त्र्यम्बकेश्वररस)


Sanskrit Shlokas
Shloka 112
Sootakasya palam pancha palanikam taamrachoornikam | Jambeeraanaam dravai: pishtam sootatalyashcha gandhakam ||112||
Take five Pala of Parada (mercury), one Pala of Tamra Churna (copper powder), Triturate with lemon juice, and add Gandhaka equal in weight to Parada.
Shloka 113
Naagavallidalaih pishtam taamrapishtam prakalpayet | Rudhvaa laghuputaih pachyaad bhooddhare yaamapanchakam ||113||
Triturate the copper paste with betel leaf juice. Seal in a Sharava and cook in a Bhudhara Puta (earth pit firing) for five Yamas (approx. 15 hours).
Shloka 114
Aadaaya choornayettulyaistrayooshanai: samamishritai: | Ardhaangakampavaataarton bhakshayeccha dvigunjhakam ||114||
After cooling, powder it and mix with equal quantity of Trikatu. Patients with Ardhangavata (hemiplegia) and Kampavata (tremors/Parkinsonism) should consume two Gunja (250 mg).

Method of Preparation (Vidhi)

2. Add Tamra Bhasma and Triturate. Then Triturate with lemon juice.
3. Give one Bhavana with Tambula Patra Svaras (betel leaf juice) and form one large ball. Dry.
4. Seal in Sharava Samput with clay and cook in Bhudhara Puta for 5 hours.
5. Next day when cooled, remove and Triturate. Add equal Trikatu Churna, Triturate well. Store in glass. This is Tryambakeshwar Ras.

Dosage & Administration
2 Ratti (250 mg) with Gau Ghrita (cow's ghee), morning and evening.

Mritamaabhrakasattvam cha kaansyam shulbam hi maakshikam | Gandhakam taalakam sarvam bhaagottaravivarddhitam ||89||
Abhraka Satva (mica essence), Kansya (bronze ash), Shulba (copper), Makshika (iron pyrite ash), Gandhaka (sulphur), and Talaka (orpiment) — all in progressively increasing proportions.
Shloka 90
Tatsarvam kajjaleekritya vaataarisnehasanyutam | Mardayetsaptadivasa goleekritya tu yatatah ||90||

Shloka 92
Prapachedvaalukaayantre yaanaanaam dvaadashaavidha | Patachoornam vidhaayetadbhaavayetadanantaram ||92||
Cook in Baluka Yantra (sand bath) for twelve Yamas (36 hours). Make fine cloth-filtered powder and give Bhavana.
Shloka 93-100
Panchakola-chitraanjuvaorunaadikashayatah | Dashamoola-kashayena shunghavera-rasena cha...
Process with decoctions of Panchakola, Chitraka Mula, and Varuna-adi Gana. Process with Dashamula Kwath and Shunthi decoction. Form shade-dried tablets. This medicine treats significantly: 80 types of Vata diseases, 8 types of Gulma, 8 types of Shula, all abdominal diseases, bloating, Anaha, Visuchika (cholera), Mandagni, Aamdosha, burning, vomiting, Grahani, asthma, cough, all worm infestations, joint stiffness, neck rigidity, diseases of horses, fever, diarrhea, hemorrhoids and Tridoshaja diseases. Named Vaatvidhvansan Ras by the great Acharya Shri Nandi. Should be consumed by those with Mandagni; after its use the patient can eat abundantly.

6. Vrikodari Vati (वृकोदरीवटी)


Sanskrit Shlokas
Shloka 101
Sootagandhakatikshnaih sataapyaih samabhaagakai | Rasaamshamaparam sarvam shatkola jeerakadvayam ||101||
Take equal parts of Parada, Gandhaka, Tiksna Loha ash, Tamra and Abhraka. Then take Shatkolam (six pungents) and two types of Jeera (cumin).
Shloka 102
Souvarchalam sasindhoothyam vidangascha haritaki | Amlavetasam sarvam beejapoora ambumardditam || Gutikaasten kalkena kaaryaah kolaasthimaatrakaah ||102||
Also include Sauvarchala Lavana, Saindhava, Vidanga, Haritaki, Amlavetak — all ground with Bijapura (citron) juice. Form tablets the size of a date stone.

This tablet formulated by the great physician Yogini, renowned throughout the three worlds, is called Vrikodari. Consumed with warm water, it rapidly treats significantly: all Vata-Dosha diseases, all Kapha-Dosha diseases, Mandagni (weak digestion), Grahani (malabsorption) and four types of Mahajirna (severe indigestion).

Etymology
Vrikodara means 'wolf's belly' — referring to an animal with extremely powerful digestive fire. It is also a synonym for Bhimsen, famed for his enormous appetite. The name signifies a medicine that kindles digestive fire to the strength of a wolf.

7. Aamvateshwar Ras (आमवातेश्वर रस)


Ingredients (Dravya)
Ingredient (Sanskrit)	English Name	Quantity

Shu. Parada (शु. पारद)	Purified Mercury	1 Tola (12 gm)

Method of Preparation (Vidhi)
1. Make Kajjali from Parada and Gandhaka. Add Bhasmas and mix.
2. Give 7 Bhavanas with Eranda Patra Ras (castor leaf juice).
3. Give 20 Bhavanas with Panchakola Kwath (Pippali, Pippali Mula, Chavya, Chitraka, Shunthi). Dry repeatedly in sunlight.
4. Give 10 Bhavanas with Giloya (Tinospora cordifolia) juice.
5. Add equal Sohaga ka Phula (purified borax), half-part Vidala Vana, Kali Mirch, Imli ka Kshar, Dantimula 1-1 Tola, Shunthi, Kali Mirch, Pippali, Harad, Baheda, Amla, Laung at 6-6 Mashe each. Triturate together. (Ref: Rasayogasaar)

Dosage & Administration
2-4 Ratti (250-500 mg) twice daily with butter or ghee. Follow with Nirgundi Ras or Eranda Mula Kwath.

Anupana for Different Conditions
- Ajirna (indigestion): Nimbu Ras or Saindhava Namak water
- Gulma (abdominal tumor): Sanjeekhara, Ghee or Suhanjana bark juice
- Aadhmana (bloating): Roasted Hing with Ghee
- Udara Roga and Shotha: Gomutra or Kutaki powder
- Medovridhi (obesity): Honey mixed water
- Panduroga (anaemia): Amla and Pippali Churna

Clinical Experience (Swanubhav)
Remarkable results in Aamvata when given with Maharasnadi Kwath. Give 1-2 Ratti in Adrakh Svaras followed by the Kwath. When urination is decreased, adding 2-6 Ratti of Yavakshar to Maharasnadi Kashaya gives benefit in smaller doses. (Ref: Shri Radhakrishna Ji Vaidya)

Therapeutic Benefits
Created by Lord Vishnu. Extremely powerful Agni-Deepak (digestive fire stimulant). Treats significantly Aamvata along with its complications. Makes obese persons lean and lean persons healthy and strong. Treats significantly all digestive system disorders. Rapidly cures both curable and incurable, acute and chronic Aamvata. Also beneficial for: Gulma, Arsha, Grahani, Shotha, Pandu, Udara Roga and more.

Scholarly Commentary (Vivechan)
This formulation is Kshar-Lavana Pradhana (alkali and salt predominant), strongly stimulating gastric juices. The Tamra-Parada combination further stimulates liver secretion. This kindles Agni strongly, burning Mandagni, Aamavridhi, Sendriya Visha and Keetanu-janya disease groups.
Note: In severe acute Aamvata with fever 102-106°F, a strongly toxic-eliminating purgative should be given. In such cases, Brihat Sinhanada Guggulu or Aamvatapramathini with Nisoth Kwath is preferred. After the acute phase resolves, Aamvateshwar Ras is more useful in the chronic stage.

Consumed with black pepper and ghee for half a Mandala (14 days), it cures: Vatadi Maharoga, Shwasa (asthma), Kaasa (cough) and Kshaya (tuberculosis).

Scholarly Commentary (Vivechan)
Vataprakopa Shamak (pacifier of aggravated Vata), Antrashhodhak (intestinal cleanser), Shaktivardhak (strength builder) and Agnipradeepak (digestive fire stimulant). Main ingredients are essentially the same as Mahavaatvidhvansan Ras but with less Bachhnaag. Bhavana here is only with Ghee Kunvar (Aloe vera) for intestinal purification, creating a slight difference in function.
Mahavaatvidhvansan Ras primarily acts on nerve channels and blood vessels. Due to high Bachhnaag, it is not for weak-hearted Aamvata patients. Vaatgajendrasinh does not have this concern and also contains cardiac-strengthening Loha Bhasma and Abhraka Bhasma, making it safer and effective in Aamvata.
Note on Bachhnaag: Eliminates toxins through urine and perspiration, reduces fever, immediately relieves pain and increases strength. It has direct pacifying effect on nerve channels. If given beyond body tolerance, it can harm the heart and blood vessels. Therefore medicines containing Bachhnaag should always be given in low doses.
